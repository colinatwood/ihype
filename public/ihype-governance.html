02 · Economics
Ticket fee structure
Every dollar is accounted for before a ticket is sold. The split is set per show by the creator — the platform takes nothing.
45%
Headliner
Default artist share. Adjustable per show at creation time.
45%
Venue
Default venue share. Covers hosting, production, and door costs.
10%
Promoter
Goes to the affiliate promoter who drove the ticket sale — or rolls into the general promoter pool.
0%
iHYPE
The platform takes no cut of ticket revenue in beta. Period.
Context: Ticketmaster + Live Nation charge buyers 20–30% in fees on top of face value. Primary platforms routinely extract 5–15% from creators before the buyer fee is added. iHYPE's model routes 100% of face value to the people who ran the event — and publishes the exact split on every show page.
03 · Algorithm
Recommendation weights
The ranking formula is fixed code, not a hidden auction. No profile can pay to rank higher. The weights are published here and enforced in the API.
Score = taste×0.40 + geo×0.25 + social×0.20 + momentum×0.15
Null signals are excluded from the weight denominator — partial profiles still rank fairly.

Geo uses a 4-tier distance cascade (local ×1.0 → regional ×0.55 → national ×0.20 → global). Momentum is hype velocity per day, log-normalized. No paid signals. No behavioral targeting.

What this means in practice

No paid placementArtists cannot buy a higher rank. Genre fit and proximity always outweigh popularity when they're in conflict.
Local bias is intentionalThe geo weight means a local artist with 12 HYPE can outrank a national act with 5,000 in the local feed.

05 · Governance
Community board structure
Platform decisions are governed by a 9-seat board designed to prevent any single stakeholder — including investors — from controlling the product direction.
3 × Artist seats
Elected by verified artist accounts. Serve 2-year terms. Majority required to change fee structure or ranking weights.
2 × Venue seats
Elected by verified venue accounts. Represent the live-event side of the marketplace.
2 × Fan seats
Elected from fan accounts. Ensure the listener perspective is represented in product decisions.
1 × Promoter seat
Elected by verified promoter accounts. Covers the touring and curation economy.
1 × Independent seat
Appointed by board consensus. Must have no financial stake in iHYPE. Provides outside accountability.
0 × Investor seats
Capital does not come with governance rights. Investors receive economic return only — no board seat, no veto, no product vote.
Beta status: The board election system will launch with the platform's public release. During the beta period, founding team decisions are subject to community RFC (Request for Comment) before implementation. This governance page is itself an RFC artifact — changes to it are logged in the ledger above.
